How To Look After A Cactus Plant

Providing a general tip to caring for a cactus or succulent group is not an easy task. Cactus plant comes in a unique shape. They can be in small, delicate to large or more striking and as a result, there will always be a cactus plant to suit the comfort of every home. The cactus plant can survive in a dry environment such as desert land. Also, the cactus can retain water for a longer period. However, it is not an easy task to look after the cactus plant.

Choosing A Cactus

Cactus plants are succulents by nature. The factor to consider when choosing cacti is their areoles which are not proving to be in succulents. Identifying the specific species of the cactus to keep is very important. For an instant, most succulents grow in low moisture, sunny climate and high temperature as seen in the desert and some succulent such as Epiphyllum grow in the rainforest. It is important to be mindful of the environmental factor in which the succulent thrive and ensure that the best possible condition to enhance growth are met to achieve the best possible result.

Creating The Ideal Environment For Cactus

Having identified your cactus type, the next step is to create the right environment for it. select an open and free draining pot to prevent waterlogging and best recreate the ideal habitat for your succulent. Cacti and succulents are okay to be kept on a windowsill for a long period (all year round in the most part), however, certain species need to be stored in a semi-shade environment e.g Rhipsalis. Maintaining the requirements of your cactus is paramount to its sustainability. The ideal temperature of the cactus plant at night is a minimum of 8-10°C (46-50°F).
